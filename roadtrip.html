ROAD TRIP TO "DRIES UP QUICKLY" CITY OF MATI, DAVAO ORIENTAL

February 19, 2023 /

We last visited Mati, Davao Oriental, months ago. Yet I can still clearly remember the adventure we had, especially on the trip there. You might want to think about visiting the City of Mati in the province of Davao Oriental in addition to touring Davao City and Samal Island. It is also well recognized for being a beach resort. The following is the text from the article.

A LITTLE ADVENTURE ON OUR WAY TO MATI

After seeing Samal Island as part of our Davao itinerary, we drove the about three to four hours to Mati from Davao City. Waze and Google Maps were our family because I am not familiar with the roads and this is my first time driving around in this area.

The Pan-Philippine Highway was reached after we traveled the Davao City-Panabon City Road. We were supposed to turn right on Surigao-Davao Coastal Road, but our map probably wanted us to have a little adventure.

Until we reached Timog Avenue, then to Apokon Road, and eventually to Surigao-Davao Coastal Road, we were directed to a little area in Tagum with winding, uneven, and muddy roads. Umikot kami at napalayo, to put it simply. .

Along the Surigao-Davao Coastal Road, which will pass by some Davao de Oro municipalities, our second leg of the journey began (formerly known as Compostela Valley). I was first taking in the music, the kulitan with family, and the mountainous scenery (be prepared for curve/zigzag roads) when it started to pour so heavily. Zero visibility existed. Consider driving in an unfamiliar place in a lightly inhabited area with a strong rain. Isn't that worrisome? I believed I was in charge of ensuring everyone's security.

My study indicates that the province typically experiences year-round rain and warm, muggy temperatures. We are blessed to be safe. At some time, it became dry, and as we continued traveling, the sun came out.

QUICK STOP AT THE DINOSAUR SLEEPING VIEW DECK

Upon leaving Davao City, we made our first stop at the Sleeping Dinosaur View Deck. It is a well-known rest stop on the road to Badas Point, Brgy, which is where the City of Mati is situated. Dawan. Around Pujada Bay, you may observe an island that resembles a dinosaur dozing peacefully on its belly. The view is really lovely and peaceful from the deck. Before getting back on the road, we snapped a few pictures of the island. There aren't many vendors selling snacks either.

By the way, before reaching the sleeping dinosaur view deck, you will pass by Davao Oriental Arc which you can stop by for a photo op as well.

THE CITY OF MATI

We arrived in the city proper after many hours of travel. I find the city to be quite tranquil. Despite being a city, it has a somewhat relaxed vibe. It has its charm. Sincerily, I didn't have high hopes. I am feeling because of her liblib. It's a great way to start your day. It's a great way to start your day. Also, there were services from Food Panda and Grab Food.

We stopped by Ciangi Restaurant for our very late lunch. Ciangi is a known restaurant in Mati. It is situated near the bay walk area. The restaurant is spacious, and the food is affordable. I just noticed that in Mati, ginger is mostly present in their local dishes (or maybe it’s a staple in the region because I also noticed that when we were in Samal).

After which, we explored the bay walk area. The locals were very friendly. With their warm smiles, they offered to take pictures for us. I even had a little chat with those who were stationed at the entrance of the bay walk area. They recommended some things that we can do and places to check out in their city. We also rode a tricycle to explore the city even just for a very short period of time. We came across a fruit-cart vendor to which we bought some of his lanzones.

CHECK-IN AT OK HOTEL RESORT

After exploring the city, we hit the road again before it nearly gets dark. We went straight to OK HOTEL RESORT to check-in. The resort is located along Dahican-Bobon Road in Mati, and it is approximately 14 kilometers from the city proper.

A NOT-SO EARLY MORNING DIP AT DAHICAN BEACH

The following day, we headed to Dahican Beach for a morning swim, which is only 30 minutes away from OK HOTEL. Dahican Beach is a 7-km stretch of white sand and blue sea that is formed like a crescent. As it strikes the shoreline, large waves may be seen. It is understandable why Dahican is a popular surfing and skimboarding location. You can rent a surfboard and pay a surf teacher if you want to learn how to surf.

GETTING TO MATI CITY

  1. Plan a trip to Davao City. Daily flights to Davao are provided by Cebu Pacific, Philippine Airlines, and Air Asia.
  2. Go to Ecoland Transport Terminal from Davao International Airport to get a bus to Mati, Davao Oriental.
  3. At the Mati City Bus Terminal, get off. From there, you may get on a tricycle and travel the city at will. If you're going from Mati to nearby towns, jeepneys are also an option.
